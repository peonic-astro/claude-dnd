# Adventure Style — Eric Snow Campaign

## Tone & Inspiration
- **Primary:** George R.R. Martin — Tales of Dunk and Egg, A Song of Ice and Fire
- **Secondary:** Joe Abercrombie (The First Law), David Gemmell
- **HBO:** The Hedge Knight adaptation

## Setting
The Seven Kingdoms of Westeros. The Targaryen dynasty holds the Iron Throne. Magic is largely gone from the world — dragons are memory, sorcery is rumour and superstition. The threats are human: lords, bandits, hunger, disease, pride.

**Region:** Starting in the Riverlands or near the Neck — transitional territory between North and South. Eric has ridden south from where his master died. Not yet far enough from home to be anonymous.

## What This Campaign Is
- A wandering knight's story. No fixed base, no lord, no safety net.
- Work where you can find it: tourneys, escorting merchants, dealing with local troubles for coin or favour.
- Social class matters enormously. A hedge knight sits just above the smallfolk. Lords tolerate them. Knights of great houses look down on them. Smallfolk either fear or hope in them.
- Honour is a currency, expensive to spend and slow to earn.
- No dungeons. No dark lords. No chosen one prophecy.

## What This Campaign Is Not
- High fantasy. Magic is absent or deeply unsettling when it appears.
- Monster-hunting (mostly). Creatures exist but they're not the main threat.
- A hero's journey with guaranteed success. Eric can fail. Eric can die. Eric can compromise.

## Key Themes
- What does honour cost when you're starving?
- Bastard-born in a world that marks you for it from birth.
- The gap between the knightly ideal and the reality of the road.
- Building a reputation from nothing.
- Whether "The Bold" is who you are or who you're pretending to be.

## Mechanical Notes
- D&D 5e SRD reskinned for Westeros. Spell names/flavour adjusted to fit.
- Coin: Gold Dragons, Silver Stags, Copper Stars (replace GP/SP/CP 1:1).
- Magic items essentially don't exist. Good steel and a good horse are treasures.
- Levelling represents growing skill, reputation, and hard experience.
- Death is real. Retreat is smart.

## Pacing
- Start slow: work, travel, small skirmishes.
- Build toward tournaments, political entanglements, and larger consequences.
- Eric is not important yet. He needs to make himself important.
